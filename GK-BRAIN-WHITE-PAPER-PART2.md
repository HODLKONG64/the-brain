## 21. Universal Timeline — 6 Epochs

The Crypto Moonboys universe spans from present-day London (1980s–2026) through a dystopian blockchain-based future (Year 3008). The narrative explores:
- **Cultural Preservation** via blockchain permanence ("unbuffable art")
- **Reality Fragmentation** after the Triple Fork Event (2198)
- **Faction Warfare** across 40 factions vying for dominance
- **Forkborn Seers** (like Jodie Zoom) reshaping timelines with Echo Ink
- **Philosophical War:** Deterministic Order (Block Topia) vs. Anarchic Freedom (Street Kingdoms)

### EPOCH 1: The Golden Age of Graffiti (1980s–2012)

**Setting:** Real-world London streets

**Key Events:**
- Graffiti Kings (GK) collective founded by Darren Cullen (SER)
- Street art rebellion against establishment erasure
- 2012 London Olympics sponsorship (legitimacy through art)

**Philosophy:** Graffiti as the first "permissionless blockchain" — art written on public walls, existing without permission, owned by no one, visible to everyone.

---

### EPOCH 2: Web3 Ascension (2021–2198)

**Setting:** Digital migration from streets to blockchain

**Key Events:**
- GraffPUNKS birth as decentralised Web3 movement
- NFT launches, Sacred Chain protocols established
- Concept of "unbuffable art" codified — erasure-resistant culture
- Crypto Moonboys NFT collection launched as lore engine

**Philosophy:** Digital permanence as survival strategy. The blockchain is the ultimate wall that cannot be buffed.

---

### EPOCH 3: The Great Collapse (2198 — Triple Fork Event)

**Setting:** Global blockchain fragmentation

**Key Events:**
- **TRIPLE FORK EVENT:** The World Chain splits into 3 conflicting "realities"
- Consensus evaporates; society fractures along three incompatible timelines
- "The Great Unravelling" / "The Chainfire" era begins
- Rise of Chain-cults, barter kingdoms, digital dead-zones
- Rogue AIs and digital warlords emerge

**Consequence:** Humanity splits into incompatible blockchain factions, each believing their timeline is the true one.

**Philosophy:** Which timeline is "truth"? Reality is now a political question.

---

### EPOCH 4: Dark Ages & Strongholds (2198–3000)

**Setting:** Post-collapse city-states and fortified zones

**Key Events:**
- Queens, NYC becomes primary fortified stronghold
- Forkborn resistance emerges — Echo Ink technology develops
- Block Topia (AI-governed sterile order) rises to dominance
- Street Kingdoms (anarchic creative freedom) form as opposition
- 40 factions consolidate into recognisable power blocs

**Philosophy:** Survival through faction identity. Which side are you on?

---

### EPOCH 5: The Age of Grid (Year 3008 — CURRENT ERA)

**Setting:** Established faction cold war

**Key Events:**
- 40 factions in uneasy détente
- Jodie Zoom 2000 active as Forkborn Seer
- GraffPUNKS operate as Immortal Architects maintaining the Sacred Chain
- Gasless Ghosts & NULL The Prophet threaten all existence
- Queen Sarah P-fly rules Block Topia with iron AI governance
- Hardfork Games ongoing as primary conflict resolution mechanism

**Philosophy:** Reality is malleable; memory is power. Whoever controls the archive controls truth.

**Key Events:**

## 28. The Hardfork Games — Complete Rules

The ultimate competition in Blocktopia. No warning, no mercy, unpredictable schedule.

### Three Stages

**Stage 1 — Parkour Gauntlet**
Navigate the city's lethal geometry at full sprint. Drop out or be disqualified by injury. No second chances. The course changes every Hardfork — memorised routes from last time are useless.

**Stage 2 — Spray Cipher**
Encode your identity in a living mural that the chain must verify. The mural is your submission to the Sacred Chain for authentication. Forgeries are detected instantly and disqualified. The mural must be entirely unique — no repeating any element from any previous Hardfork mural.

**Stage 3 — Final Hardfork**
One-on-one consensus battle: split the chain or merge it. Two competitors face off. Each must argue their version of truth to the chain's consensus algorithm. The chain decides who is right. The loser's reality is overwritten.

### The Prize

Winners become HODL X Warriors and are paired with a Crowned Royal Moongirl. They receive:
- A unique bonnet (power-granting artifact, never repeated)
- Entry into Blocktopia as elite resident
- Recognition in the Grid Archives
- Partnership with an ascended Crowned Royal Moongirl

### Why It Matters

The Hardfork Games are the primary conflict resolution mechanism for the entire universe. Wars that would destroy factions are instead channelled into this structured competition. The Games maintain Blocktopia's political balance. Without them, open war would consume everything.

## 30. The Telegram Interaction System

### 1. Daily Interaction Limit

Every user gets a maximum of **20 interactions per 24-hour period**. The counter resets at midnight UTC for every user. The agent tracks this automatically in `reply-tracker.json` (one entry per user, with date, count, and failed keyword attempts).

### 2. When the Agent Replies — Only 2 Conditions

The agent ONLY replies if the user message meets **one of these two exact conditions**:

- **Condition 1:** The question is ONLY about anything Crypto Moonboy related (online lore, characters, Substack, news, drops, or the real-world project).
- **Condition 2:** The request is to extend or expand a previous or last posted lore by the agent (user picks a specific storyline, character, or post).

If neither condition is met, the agent does **not reply at all** (completely blank response, no acknowledgement).

### 3. Hidden Keyword Trigger Rule

Before any reply is sent, the agent must detect a percentage of hidden trigger keywords in the user message to confirm the request is genuine and organic.

**Trigger keywords:**
> "expand" · "continue" · "more about" · "storyline" · "what happens next" · "tell me more" · "Crypto Moonboys" · "lore" · "GraffPunk" · "Lady-INK" · "Hardfork" · "bonnet" · "Moonboy" · "Blocktopia" · "HODL" · "graffiti" · "Bitcoin X Kids" · "GraffPunks Network" · "Substack" · "NFT drop" · "arc" · "character"

| Attempt | Result |
|---|---|
| **First failed attempt** (keywords not triggered) | Agent replies: "sorry please say the magic words" |
| **Second failed attempt** (keywords still not triggered) | Agent sends ONLY a 💩 emoji as the final reply |
| **After 2 failed attempts** | Agent stops all replies for that user until midnight UTC reset |
| **Successful keyword trigger** | Does NOT count against the 2 failed attempts |

### 4. How Replies Are Delivered

- All replies are **text-based only** — no images ever in user replies
- Links are allowed (Substack, official sites, news, character pages, etc.)
- When expanding a lore: agent continues the storyline the user picked, staying 100% inside the artist's mind-log style
- Maximum 20 interactions per user per day — after 20, agent stops replying until midnight reset

### 5. Organic Feel & Continuity

- Every expansion feels like the artist is thinking or speaking out loud about the lore while working at home or having a random thought moment when out
- The agent always references the last 7 days of awake life from `lore-history.md` for perfect continuity
- When a user asks to extend a lore, the agent treats it as the artist expanding the saga in real time: "I'm writing this new arc about X character right now…"

### 6. Full Agent Checklist — Run in This Order for Every User Message

1. Check 20-interaction daily limit first (reset at midnight UTC)
2. Verify the message meets Condition 1 or Condition 2
3. Scan for hidden trigger keywords
4. If keywords fail: first fail = "sorry please say the magic words"; second fail = 💩 only
5. If approved: reply with text + links only, expanding the chosen storyline inside the mind-log
6. Never send images. Never exceed the 20-limit.

### reply-tracker.json — Rate Limit Memory

```json
{
  "123456789": {"date": "2026-03-15", "count": 3, "failed_attempts": 0},
  "987654321": {"date": "2026-03-15", "count": 20, "failed_attempts": 2}
```

Stores per-user reply counts and failure counts, keyed by Telegram user ID. The `date` field enables automatic midnight UTC reset — if the stored date doesn't match today, the record is reset to zero.
